History of Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL)

Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL) was incorporated in January 1972 as a Public Sector Undertaking to operate coking coal mines in the Jharia and Raniganj Coalfields, which were taken over by the Government of India on 16th October 1971. The objective was to ensure the planned development of the scarce coking coal resources in the country, which are vital for the steel sector and other core industries.

Background

Prior to nationalization, most of the coking coal mines in the Jharia and Raniganj coalfields were operated by private owners. The unscientific mining practices, lack of investment in technology, and poor safety standards led to wastage of coal, frequent accidents, and environmental degradation. The Government of India, recognizing the strategic importance of coking coal for the steel industry, decided to nationalize these mines.

Nationalization and Formation
  • On 16th October 1971, the Government of India took over 214 coking coal mines and 12 coke ovens in the Jharia and Raniganj coalfields.
  • Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL) was incorporated in January 1972 to manage these mines and ensure their scientific and planned development.
  • BCCL became a subsidiary of Coal India Limited (CIL) after the formation of CIL in November 1975.
Growth and Development
  • BCCL started with 214 coking coal mines, which were later reorganized and merged for better management and efficiency.
  • The company introduced modern mining technologies, improved safety standards, and invested in coal beneficiation through washeries.
  • BCCL played a crucial role in supplying prime coking coal to the steel sector, which is essential for the country’s industrial growth.
Current Status
  • BCCL operates 36 coal mines (11 underground, 16 open-cast, and 9 mixed mines) in the Jharia and Raniganj coalfields.
  • The company manages 8 coal washeries, workshops, hospitals, and educational institutions for employees and their families.
  • BCCL is the major producer of prime coking coal in India, catering to the requirements of the steel sector and other core industries.
Legacy and Contribution
  • BCCL has contributed significantly to the planned and scientific development of coking coal resources in India.
  • The company has played a pivotal role in supporting the steel industry and the nation’s economic growth.
  • BCCL continues to focus on modernization, safety, environmental management, and community development.
